# ArtempireMJ Copy Concision Runbook

**Problem:** ArtempireMJ web copy slides into *storybook narration* — little vignettes ("A collector flies to Marrakech for the preview days. A reader flies home to Lagos for the biennial.") that paint a scene instead of making a claim. The best brands do not narrate. They state. This runbook documents how brand-grade web copy actually reads, names the anti-pattern, and rewrites every page to the standard.

**Constraints kept:** brand line "The world, told through artists"; warm but tight; audience-as-hero; embed-not-preach; NO em-dashes; NO negation / AI-negation; engine room (DISCOVER internals) unnamed where it adds nothing. The fix is *cut the scene, keep the answer*.

Date: 2026-06-28. Sources cited inline and at the end.

**What the numbers say:**

- **Hero headlines average 2 to 9 words.** Median ~6. Apple/Nike/Airbnb run 1 to 4. Nobody opens with a sentence longer than ~11 words.
- **Subheads are one or two short sentences, 6 to 26 words.** When there is a list, it is a list of *capabilities* (Stripe, Mailchimp, Notion), not a sequence of imagined people.
- **Zero of fourteen open with a narrative scene.** Not one starts "A person does X, then flies to Y." They open with a claim, a promise, or an imperative.
- **Proof is named, not dramatized.** Vercel cites "Notion powers millions of agent conversations daily." That is a fact, not a story. The Economist *implies* the cost of not reading via one fictional line and then stops; it never walks you through the trip.

### The 7 rules of concise brand copy

1. **Claim first, not scene first.** Open with what the thing is or does. "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue." Never "A founder in Lagos opens Stripe at 2am..."
2. **Headline 3 to 8 words.** If it needs a comma-spliced second clause, it is two ideas. Cut one.
3. **Subhead is one or two short sentences.** Say the benefit and the proof. Stop.
4. **One idea per line.** Lists are capabilities or facts, not a parade of imagined characters.
5. **Confident, not explanatory.** State it as true. "We read every one" beats "We try our best to read as many as we can."
6. **Benefit lives in the headline.** Mailchimp puts "minus the learning curve" in the H1. The reader should get the payoff before the subhead.
7. **Let the image hold the scene; let the words hold the claim.** Apple's photo is the Marrakech preview day. The copy says one confident thing. ArtempireMJ already has correspondent photography doing this work, so the copy is free to be tight.

## 2. THE ANTI-PATTERN — storybook narration

The current ArtempireMJ copy frequently paints a little movie before it says anything. Examples from the live pages:

> **Itineraries (current):** "A collector flies to Marrakech for the preview days. A reader flies home to Lagos for the biennial, back to a city their family came from. Someone plans a week around the Venice opening just to stand in front of the work."

> **Submit (current):** "A painter in Lagos, a photographer in Nairobi, a tattoo artist in São Paulo. Each one sent a few images and a paragraph about the work, and that was the whole door."

> **Home (current):** "You make art and want the next open door. You plan a trip and want the one worth taking. You want to understand the world through what people make in it."

**Why these read weaker than a direct claim:**

- **The reader has to decode before they get value.** Three sentences of other people's travel arrive before the page tells *you* what it does. By rule 1, the claim should be first.
- **It buries the benefit under fiction.** The Marrakech/Lagos/Venice trio is 40+ words of scene-setting for one idea: *"We tell you the dates early enough to book."* That idea is the product. The scene is decoration.
- **It dilutes confidence.** Naming three hypothetical travellers reads like the brand is *arguing* the trip is worth it. A confident brand asserts it: "Be there the week the art is. Here are the dates." Patagonia does not narrate a melting glacier; it says "We're in business to save our home planet."
- **It is slower than the eye.** Hero copy is scanned, not read. A vignette has no scannable claim. "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue" lands in one beat; "A founder flies to..." lands in none.
- **It competes with the photography.** ArtempireMJ already shows the FNB Art Joburg floor shot by a real correspondent. The image *is* the scene. Narrating a second imagined scene in words is redundant and weaker than the real photo.

The vignette is not wrong because it is warm. It is weaker because it makes the reader build the meaning instead of handing it to them. Keep the warmth; move it into a confident claim.

## 3. REWRITES — every page

Rule of thumb applied: H1 3 to 8 words, lede 1 to 2 short sentences, anxiety-answers said directly (artists: free / confirmed / we read every one; travellers: the dates and the breadth). No vignettes, no em-dashes, no negation.

### Home (`index.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `The world, told through artists.`

**Lede — before:**
> You make art and want the next open door. You plan a trip and want the one worth taking. You want to understand the world through what people make in it. ArtempireMJ gathers it, keeps it current, and shows you the way in.

**Lede — after:**
> The open calls worth applying to. The trips worth taking. The artists worth knowing. ArtempireMJ gathers the art world's moving parts, verifies them, and shows you the way in.

*(33 words, three parallel claims, benefit up front, no "you make art / you plan a trip" framing delay.)*

**Calendar section lede — before:**
> What is open right now: open calls, residencies, grants, and fairs, across the Americas, Europe, Africa, and Asia and the Middle East. Each one verified before it is listed, fresh every week, with the link to apply. When you ask an assistant what is on in the art world, this is the answer it should give you. Here, you can act on it.

**After:**
> What is open right now, worldwide: open calls, residencies, grants, and fairs. Each one verified before it is listed, refreshed weekly, with the link to apply.

*(Cuts the "when you ask an assistant" meta-aside, which explains rather than claims.)*

**Newsletter H2 (keep):** `The week in world art, in your inbox.`

**Newsletter body — before:**
> The open doors before they close. The makers worth knowing. The trips worth taking. One email a week, free. It is the surest way to see what is on before everyone else.

**After (keep, tightened):**
> The open doors before they close. The makers worth knowing. The trips worth taking. One email a week, free.

*(The "surest way" line is a soft brag that explains; the three fragments already carry it.)*

### Itineraries (`itineraries/index.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `Be there the week the art is.`

**Lede — before:**
> A collector flies to Marrakech for the preview days. A reader flies home to Lagos for the biennial, back to a city their family came from. Someone plans a week around the Venice opening just to stand in front of the work. You can travel that way too, to wherever the art pulls you. Here is what is worth crossing the world for, gathered by city, with the dates early enough to book before the rooms fill. The calendar holds the dates. Itineraries hold the way in: what to see, who to meet, where the art actually is.

**Lede — after:**
> The fairs and biennials worth crossing the world for, gathered by city. We give you the dates early enough to book before the rooms fill, and the way in once you land: what to see, who to meet, where the art actually is.

*(The three-traveller vignette is gone. The traveller's anxiety-answers stay, said directly: dates early, book before rooms fill, what to see / who to meet / where.)*

**"Built around the calendar" — before:**
> An itinerary starts from a real date on the Global Art Calendar, a fair in Cape Town or a biennial in Sharjah, and builds the trip around it: what to see, who to meet, where the art actually is. The diaspora traveller flying in for the fair is a reader we plan for by name.

**After:**
> Every itinerary starts from a real date on the Global Art Calendar, a fair in Cape Town or a biennial in Sharjah, and builds the trip around it. Diaspora travellers flying in for the fair are readers we plan for.

*(Keeps the named cities as concrete proof, drops the "by name" flourish that over-explains.)*

### Submit (`submit.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `Your work belongs in front of the world.`

**Lede — before:**
> A painter in Lagos, a photographer in Nairobi, a tattoo artist in São Paulo. Each one sent a few images and a paragraph about the work, and that was the whole door. You carry the story too, in any medium: paint, photograph, sculpture, music, ink, writing, design, craft. Tell us what you are making and what the world should see through it. It costs nothing to send, wherever you live, and we read every one.

**Lede — after:**
> Any medium counts: paint, photograph, sculpture, music, ink, writing, design, craft. Send a few images and a paragraph about the work. That is the whole door. It costs nothing, wherever you live, and we read every one.

*(The Lagos/Nairobi/São Paulo roster is cut; the breadth-of-medium list already proves "artists everywhere." All three artist anxiety-answers kept and front-loaded: low effort, free, we read every one.)*

**Second paragraph — before:**
> A feature puts your name and your work in front of an audience that came here to find people like you. See who is on the stage. If your story is right for us, we will write back, and you could be next.

**After (keep, tightened):**
> A feature puts your name and your work in front of an audience that came here to find people like you. See who is on the stage. If your story is right for us, we will write back.

*("you could be next" is a sales tag the confident version drops.)*

### Discover (`discover/index.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `DISCOVER`

**Lede — before:**
> Find the door before it closes. The opportunity radar: open calls, residencies, grants, and fairs, gathered for the artists who want them. Every date is confirmed before it is listed, so you can trust what you act on. Free to read, wherever you live and whatever you earn, and built to stay open.

**After:**
> Find the door before it closes. Open calls, residencies, grants, and fairs, gathered for the artists who want them. Every date is confirmed before it is listed. Free to read, wherever you live.

*(Drops "so you can trust what you act on" — confirmed-before-listed already says that. Already strong page; this is a trim, not a rebuild.)*

**Lede — before:**

> This is what a feature is for: a maker, named, with the work they carry, where an audience can find them. Make art in any medium? Send us your story. If it is right for us, your name belongs here too.

*(Light. Already claim-shaped.)*

### Spotlight (`spotlight/index.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `Spotlight`

**Lede — before:**
> News, analysis, and artist features through the maker's eye. When something happens in the world, Spotlight finds the artist who carries it and tells it through them.

**After (keep):** unchanged. This is already a clean claim + mechanism, two sentences, no vignette. Model page.

### About (`about.astro`)

**H1 (keep):** `The world, told through artists.`

**Lede — before:**
> You already follow the world. The elections and the currencies, the migrations and the music, the things that move a place. ArtempireMJ brings it to you through the artists who live inside those stories. The world is what you came for. The artist is how you see it new.

**After:**
> You already follow the world: the elections, the currencies, the migrations, the music. ArtempireMJ brings it to you through the artists who live inside those stories. The world is what you came for. The artist is how you see it new.

## 4. CONCISION CHECKLIST (run before shipping any ArtempireMJ copy)

Ship only when every box is checked.

- [ ] **H1 is 3 to 8 words** and states a claim, promise, or imperative.
- [ ] **Lede is 1 to 2 short sentences.** If it is three or more, cut to the load-bearing one.
- [ ] **No vignette.** Does any sentence describe a hypothetical person doing a sequence of actions ("A collector flies to...", "A painter in Lagos sent...")? If yes, delete it and state the underlying claim directly.
- [ ] **Claim before context.** The first sentence says what this is / does for *you*, not what someone else did.
- [ ] **Benefit is in the headline or first line,** not buried after setup.
- [ ] **Anxiety-answers present and direct.** Artists: free / low-effort / we read every one. Travellers: the dates, early enough to book, and the breadth. Stated, not narrated.
- [ ] **One idea per line.** Lists are capabilities or facts, never a roster of imagined people.
- [ ] **Confident, not pleading.** Cut "surest way", "you could be next", "so you can trust", "we try our best". State it as true.
- [ ] **No em-dashes. No negation / AI-negation.** Engine-room internals unnamed unless the name earns its place (DISCOVER as a department name is fine; explaining "when you ask an assistant" is not).
- [ ] **Let the photo hold the scene.** If a correspondent image already shows the place, the words do not re-describe it.

**One-line test:** read the H1 and first sentence aloud. If a listener could not say what ArtempireMJ does for them, it is still narrating. Rewrite to the claim.
