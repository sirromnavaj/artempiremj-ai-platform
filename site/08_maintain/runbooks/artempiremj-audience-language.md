# ArtempireMJ — Audience Language: What They Want, and Copy That Lands

> Purpose: rewrite the site copy that speaks to the two core ArtempireMJ audiences in the words they
> already value. Audience-as-hero. Meet them in what they already want (a face before the argument,
> warm, specific, no em-dashes, no negation framing, the engine never named).
>
> Date: 2026-06-28 · Status: research complete, rewrites ready to lift into the pages.
>
> Voice rules these rewrites obey: second-person, audience is the hero, a real person before any
> claim, plain affirmative sentences (say what is, never frame by what it is not), no em-dashes, no
> "this isn't just X, it's Y" construction, the machine/algorithm/funnel never appears on the page.

The two audiences and their pages:
- **Audience 1 — the culturally-curious art traveller.** Page: `src/pages/itineraries/index.astro`.
- **Audience 2 — the artist, any medium.** Pages: `src/pages/submit.astro`, `src/pages/discover/index.astro`, `src/pages/artists/index.astro`.

## 1. THE TRAVELLER — the culturally-curious art traveller

### 1a. What they really want (ranked, cited)

1. **An experience that changes them, not a sight they tick off.** They protect experience spend above almost everything: the experiences market is potentially **>$3 trillion**, **52% of Gen Z splurge on experiences**, and they cut flights, shopping and food before cutting the experience itself. ([McKinsey, The evolving role of experiences in travel](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/travel/our-insights/the-evolving-role-of-experiences-in-travel))
2. **Authentic local culture, with their money reaching the local community.** In a 27,000-traveller, 33-country survey, **77% seek experiences representative of local culture** and **73% want their money to go back to the local community**. ([Booking.com 2025 Travel Predictions](https://news.booking.com/bookingcoms-2025-research-reveals-growing-traveler-awareness-of-tourism-impact-on-communities-both-at-home-and-abroad/))
3. **A reason and a date — to go *because* something is happening there.** Event-led "live travel" (trips built around festivals, fairs, cultural events) is valued at **$2.9 trillion as of 2025**; **66% say a screen or a place they saw shaped a trip** ("set-jetting"). ([Skift, the rise of experience-led travel](https://skift.com/2025/12/05/concerts-sports-and-the-rise-of-experience-led-travel/) · [Expedia Unpack '25](https://www.expedia.com/magazine/travel-trends-unpack-25/set-jetting-forecast/))
4. **To be there first, with access an outsider can't reach.** Fairs are now **35% of dealer sales** and the serious buyer flies in for the **invite-only preview/VIP days** before the public, attending **fewer, more selective** fairs (four in 2023 vs five in 2019). ([Art Basel & UBS Art Market Report 2026](https://theartmarket.artbasel.com/download/The-Art-Basel-and-UBS-Art-Market-Report-2026-by-Arts-Economics.pdf) · [Survey of Global Collecting 2023](https://theartmarket.artbasel.com/download/The-Art-Basel-and-UBS-Survey-of-Global-Collecting-in-2023.pdf) · [1-54 Marrakech 2026 preview](https://africanscolumn.com/what-to-expect-at-the-2026-1-54-marrakech/))
5. **The diaspora traveller wants a homecoming, not a holiday.** A biennial gives the return a reason and a date; "when a British-Nigerian dances at Afrochella, it's more than tourism; it's a homecoming," driven by "the desire to uncover one's heritage." ([Diaspora tourism: coming home](https://lisaiscooking.substack.com/p/diaspora-tourism-why-more-africans-abroad-are-coming-home) · [Time Out, heritage tourism boom](https://www.timeout.com/news/heritage-tourism-is-leading-to-an-african-travel-boom-heres-why-black-travellers-are-embracing-the-trend-031326) · [1-54 Marrakech](https://www.1-54.com/marrakech/))

### 1b. Their anxieties (what the copy must quietly answer)

- **Missing the week.** A biennial has fixed preview and closing dates; opening-week rooms double and sell out, so "savvy visitors book months in advance." Getting the week wrong cannot be undone. ([Venice Biennale 2026 info](https://www.labiennale.org/en/art/2026/information) · [Venice Insider Guide](https://www.veniceinsiderguide.com/venice-art-biennale-2026-tickets/))
- **Being "just a tourist," not an insider.** **45% worry they are not having an authentic experience; 62% feel a trip is wasted without the local culture; 51% rate experiencing a place "as a local" a high priority.** ([GetYourGuide / Wakefield survey](https://www.getyourguide.press/blog/2023-travel-trends-u-s-travelers-aim-to-experience-destinations-as-locals-and-crave-more-authenticity))
- **Not knowing the scene.** The value sits behind preview days, gallery dinners and which openings on which night, opaque to an outsider. ([1-54 Marrakech 2026](https://africanscolumn.com/what-to-expect-at-the-2026-1-54-marrakech/))
- **The diaspora-specific fear of "returning correctly,"** of being a visitor in one's own heritage. ([D+C, Ghana homecoming](https://www.dandc.eu/en/article/ghanas-homecoming-tourism-industry-offers-diasporans-travel-their-ancestors-country))

### 1c. The words that convert (verbatim, from proven travel copy)

Pulled from live brand pages as the proven register (do not copy them outright; match the register):
- **Black Tomato:** "how you want to feel," "getting closer, going deeper," "absorbing the places you visit," "from its experts on the ground." ([blacktomato.com](https://www.blacktomato.com/us/))
- **Atlas Obscura:** "hidden wonders," "remarkable places," "behind-the-scenes access," "wonder and curiosity." ([atlasobscura.com/about](https://www.atlasobscura.com/about))
- **Booking.com / Hilton / GetYourGuide:** "authentic experiences representative of local culture," "maximize every moment," "as a local," "more than mere tourists." ([Booking.com](https://news.booking.com/bookingcoms-2025-research-reveals-growing-traveler-awareness-of-tourism-impact-on-communities-both-at-home-and-abroad/) · [Hilton 2025 Trends](https://stories.hilton.com/releases/hilton-travel-trends-2025) · [GetYourGuide](https://www.getyourguide.press/blog/2023-travel-trends-u-s-travelers-aim-to-experience-destinations-as-locals-and-crave-more-authenticity))
- **Diaspora register:** "homecoming," "more than tourism," "return to your roots," "the continent or the African diaspora." ([1-54](https://africanscolumn.com/what-to-expect-at-the-2026-1-54-marrakech/) · [diaspora homecoming editorial](https://lisaiscooking.substack.com/p/diaspora-tourism-why-more-africans-abroad-are-coming-home))

Dominant verbs: *go deeper, immerse, absorb, witness, be there, arrive, return, maximize.*

### 1d. Before / after — Itineraries page

**H1 — before:**
> Art Itineraries

**H1 — after:**
> Be there the week the art is.

**Lede — before:**
> You travel, and you would rather not miss the fair everyone will be talking about. Here is what is
> worth planning a trip around, by city, so the dates reach you before the flights fill. The calendar
> tells you when. Itineraries tell you how to go.

**Lede — after:**
> A collector lands in Marrakech for the preview days, while the fair still belongs to the people who
> came for it. You can travel that way too. Here is the art worth crossing the world for, gathered by
> city, with the dates early enough to book the week before the rooms fill. The calendar holds the
> dates. Itineraries hold the way in: what to see, who to meet, where the art actually is.

*(Why: opens on a real person doing the thing, in a named African art city. "Be there the week,"
"the way in," "the rooms fill" answer the three anxieties — missing the week, being an outsider,
booking late — in plain affirmative lines. No em-dash, no "you would rather not miss" negation.)*

**"What is worth the trip" framing — before:**
> ## What is worth the trip

**After:**
> ## The art worth crossing the world for

**"Built around the calendar" paragraph — before:**
> An itinerary starts from a real date on the Global Art Calendar, a fair in Cape Town or a biennial
> in Sharjah, and builds the trip around it... The diaspora traveller flying in for the fair is a
> reader we plan for by name.

**After:**
> Every itinerary begins with a real date: a fair in Cape Town, a biennial in Sharjah, the preview
> week in Venice. From there it builds the trip around the art — what to see, who to meet, the
> openings worth the night. And if you are flying home for the biennial, returning to a city your
> family came from, this is written for you by name. It is more than a trip. It is a homecoming with a
> date on it.

*(Why: keeps the diaspora reader as hero and names the homecoming motivation in their own word.
Affirmative throughout.)*

## 2. THE ARTIST — any medium, emerging to mid-career, Global-South first

### 2a. What they really want (ranked, cited)

1. **To be seen and taken seriously, when most earn almost nothing from the work.** **60% of visual artists earn under $30K/year, 21% earn $0–10K, and nearly half make only 0–10% of income from art.** Visibility and being counted are the scarce goods. ([The Creative Independent artist survey](https://thecreativeindependent.com/artist-survey/) · [NPR / NORC 2025 artist-labor survey](https://www.npr.org/2025/11/19/nx-s1-5611445/2025-norc-artist-labor-survey))
2. **Fair pay and to be paid at all.** Arts workers average **~$63K vs ~$100K** for other professionals; **82% take income from other jobs.** Artists "are often alone among those whose contributions go uncompensated." ([Macquarie / creative-work survey](https://lighthouse.mq.edu.au/article/may-2024/creative-work-doesnt-pay-the-bills-new-artists-survey) · [W.A.G.E.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Working_Artists_and_the_Greater_Economy_(W.A.G.E.)))
3. **A door that opens regardless of where they live or what they earn.** The platforms artists trust say it plainly: "free for artists to use… regardless of where they live or what they earn," for "more time for making art and less hassle." ([ArtConnect relaunch](https://www.magazine.artconnect.com/editorial/relaunch) · [ArtConnect about](https://www.artconnect.com/about))
4. **Freedom, belonging, and someone walking beside them.** The most-trusted copy promises "create freely, take artistic risks," "sanctuary," "feel supported," "with you every step of your art journey." ([Creative Capital](https://creative-capital.org) · [MacDowell](https://www.macdowell.org) · [Artists Network](http://www.artistsnetwork.com/))

### 2b. The Global-South / African artist's access gap (the differentiator)

- **Walled out by cost.** Art Basel booths reach **~€200,000**; African artists are "rarely featured," appearing "only through European and American galleries." ([Dakart News](https://dakartnews.com/2025/07/03/africas-artists-deserve-a-bigger-stage-basel-is-just-the-start/))
- **Mobility and visas, the biggest structural barrier.** Artists "secure residency acceptances only to realize they cannot afford international airfare, visa fees, and travel expenses"; one artist's own Swiss visa was rejected, barring him from the fair. ([Art Residency Africa](https://artresidencyafrica.com/travel-grants-for-international-artists-getting-to-your-african-residency/) · [Dakart News](https://dakartnews.com/2025/07/03/africas-artists-deserve-a-bigger-stage-basel-is-just-the-start/))
- **Curated through a Western lens,** narratives "shaped to fit global trends rather than authentic voices." The corrective the field respects is "reverse the flow," "outside of a western context," not charity. ([MoMAA](https://momaa.org/contemporary-african-female-artists-a-new-global-vanguard/) · [Black Rock Senegal](https://blackrocksenegal.org/our-story/))

### 2c. The anxieties that block a submission, and what builds trust

- **"Paying for the privilege of rejection."** Verbatim grievance: "Fees are a paywall on opportunity… They measure means." The fix that converts is **free and no-fee, stated as an ethic.** ([Hyperallergic](https://hyperallergic.com/why-are-we-paying-for-the-privilege-of-rejection/) · [ArtConnect no-fee filter](https://www.artconnect.com/opportunities/without-fees))
- **"Is this a scam?"** A default suspicion; the trust test is transparency and a verifiable track record. The fix that converts is **dates confirmed before listing** and a real, named human voice. Vague/recycled/corporate phrasing reads as a literal red flag. ([ArtConnect, red flags](https://www.magazine.artconnect.com/resources/red-flags-to-identify-before-applying-to-an-open-call))
- **Rejection fatigue and silence.** "Frustration and pressure increase exponentially… in fear of rejection or no response at all." The fix that converts is **"we read every one"** and a promise to write back. ([Entrythingy](https://www.entrythingy.com/blog/how-to-apply-to-art-calls))

### 2d. The words that build trust (verbatim, from platforms artists trust)

"free for artists… regardless of where they live or what they earn" · "more time for making art and less hassle" · "Fees: FREE" · "create freely, take artistic risks" · "bring bold new work into the world" · "synonymous with sanctuary" · "feel supported" · "with you every step of your art journey" · "we believe… empowering them" · "galvanize the artistic energies that exist in Africa" · "outside of a western context." ([ArtConnect](https://www.magazine.artconnect.com/editorial/relaunch) · [Creative Capital](https://creative-capital.org) · [MacDowell](https://www.macdowell.org) · [Artists Network](http://www.artistsnetwork.com/) · [Black Rock Senegal](https://blackrocksenegal.org/our-story/))

### 2e. Before / after — Submit page

**H1 — before:**
> Submit your story.

**H1 — after:**
> Your work belongs in front of the world.

**Lede — before:**
> A maker carries the story. That maker is you, in any medium. Painter, photographer, sculptor,
> musician, tattoo artist, writer, designer, craftsperson. Tell us what you are working on and what
> the world should see through it.

**Lede — after:**
> A painter in Lagos, a photographer in Nairobi, a tattoo artist in São Paulo. Each one sent a few
> images and a paragraph about the work, and that was the whole door. You carry the story too, in any
> medium: paint, photograph, sculpture, music, ink, writing, design, craft. Tell us what you are
> making and what the world should see through it. It costs nothing to send, wherever you live, and we
> read every one.

**Sub-lede — before:**
> A feature puts you in front of an audience. See who is on the stage. If your story is right for us,
> you could be next.

**Sub-lede — after:**
> A feature puts your name and your work in front of an audience that came here to find people like
> you. See who is on the stage. If your story is right for us, we will write back, and you could be
> next.

*(Why: a face before the claim, three named Global-South cities to signal "regardless of where you
live"; "costs nothing to send" answers the fee anxiety; "we read every one… we will write back"
answers rejection-silence. The success message already says "We read every one" — this makes the
page keep that promise up front.)*

### 2f. Before / after — DISCOVER lede

**Before:**
> The opportunity radar. Open calls, residencies, grants, fairs, and the doors worth knowing about,
> gathered for the artists who want them. Free, and built to stay open.

**After:**
> The opportunity radar. Open calls, residencies, grants, and fairs, gathered for the artists who want
> them. Every date is confirmed before it is listed, so you can trust what you act on. Free to read,
> wherever you live and whatever you earn, and built to stay open.

*(Why: "confirmed before it is listed" answers "is this a scam"; "wherever you live and whatever you
earn" lifts ArtConnect's proven access ethic and speaks straight to the Global-South gap.)*

### 2g. Before / after — Artists / the stage lede

**H1 — before:**
> Artists we have put in front of the world.

**H1 — after (keep, lightly warmed):**
> The makers we have put in front of the world.

**Lede — before:**
> This is what a feature is for. A maker, named, with the work they carry, where an audience can find
> them. You make art? Send us your story. If it is right, you could be here.

**Lede — after:**
> Here is what a feature is for. A maker, named, with the work they carry, where an audience can find
> them and reach them. You make art, in any medium? Send us your story. If it is right for us, your
> name belongs here too.

*(Why: keeps the artist as hero; "find them and reach them" sharpens the visibility promise; "your
name belongs here too" is warmer and affirmative where "you could be here" was tentative.)*

## 3. Language cheat-sheets (say / avoid)

### Traveller
| Say | Avoid |
|---|---|
| be there the week / the preview week / arrive first | "don't miss," "never miss," "you would rather not" (negation) |
| the way in · who to meet · where the art actually is | "tour," "package," "sightseeing," "destination" (generic) |
| homecoming · return · the city your family came from | "trip of a lifetime," "bucket list" (cliché) |
| go deeper · as a local · the art worth crossing the world for | "exclusive," "luxury" as a boast (status, not feeling) |
| a real person + a named city in the first line | leading with "Art Itineraries" as a label |
| the rooms fill / book the week early | "limited spots," "act now" (hard-sell urgency) |

### Artist
| Say | Avoid |
|---|---|
| free to send · costs nothing · wherever you live, whatever you earn | "entry fee," "submission fee," "exposure" (scam markers) |
| we read every one · we will write back | "winners announced," "finalists," "contest" (lottery framing) |
| every date confirmed before it is listed | vague/recycled/corporate phrasing (reads as a scam red flag) |
| your work · your name · in any medium · the work they carry | "content," "creators," "submissions" (extractive register) |
| a named maker in a Global-South city, first | "applicants," "candidates," database language |
| belongs in front of the world · you could be next | "you could be" with no follow-through; "we don't just..." (AI-negation) |

Shared rules for both: a real human before any claim · plain affirmative sentences · no em-dashes ·
never name the engine (algorithm, funnel, AEO, calendar machinery) · the reader is the hero, the
brand is the doorway.

## WHERE THIS IS UNCERTAIN

- **Some immersion percentages come from low-authority trade blogs** (e.g. "78% interest in cultural immersion") and are not used above. The defensible figures are Booking.com (27k sample), McKinsey, Art Basel/UBS, and GetYourGuide/Wakefield. Vendor surveys (Expedia, Hilton, Booking) corroborate direction but magnitudes are marketing-grade. ([traveller brief sources above])
- **The GetYourGuide "fear of being a tourist" stats are from late 2022** — cleanest available, but ~3.5 years old; behaviour may have shifted.
- **"Before everyone else" / "be there when"** are synthesis from preview-day + room-scarcity reality, not a single verbatim brand quote.
- **Airbnb Experiences, Condé Nast Traveler, and Culture Trip copy could not be pulled verbatim** (fetch-blocked); their "like a local / truth in travel" register is asserted from search + general knowledge, not a confirmed live pull. Re-fetch before quoting them.
- **No African-artist-specific income survey was found.** The Global-South section leans on advocacy/journalism (Dakart News, MoMAA, Art Residency Africa) and fund guidelines, often single-source per claim. Strong as direction, thin as hard data.
- **Black Rock Senegal is a diaspora (Kehinde Wiley) "reverse-the-gaze" voice,** excellent for tone but not a proxy for an emerging continental-African artist's economic position.
- **Survivorship bias:** MacDowell, Rijksakademie, Creative Capital are prestige gatekeepers whose warm copy artists trust, yet they are also the source of the rejection anxiety. ArtempireMJ's actual edge is being warm **and** low-stakes (free, "we read every one"), which those institutions are not.
